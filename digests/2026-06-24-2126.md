# Agentic AI — Executive Brief for Kore.ai Leadership (Wednesday, 24 June 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are aggressively pushing voice AI agents and agentic orchestration for CX, but many still grapple with predictable pricing, proven governance for complex enterprise use cases, and independent analyst validation in the core conversational AI for CX category.

## Executive summary

1.  Five9's significant launch of Voice AI Agents directly challenges Kore.ai's voice capabilities by targeting IVR replacement and agentic self-service, yet their underlying seat-based model and analyst-flagged weaknesses remain exploitable for TCO and scalability.
2.  IBM and Parloa are expanding their agentic AI offerings for CX through strategic partnerships and market research, but both face questions around analyst leadership, pricing predictability, and global enterprise scope compared to Kore.ai's established position.
3.  Horizontal players like Glean and Salesforce Agentforce continue to focus on internal or adjacent enterprise use cases, reinforcing Kore.ai's differentiated position as a purpose-built, analyst-validated Leader for customer-facing agentic AI with robust omnichannel capabilities.

*   Arm sales with clear TCO models comparing Kore.ai's predictable cost-per-outcome against Five9's seat-based + AI overage model, especially for voice, to highlight the long-term economic advantage.
*   Reinforce Kore.ai's consistent Leader positioning across Gartner and Forrester for Conversational AI in CX, contrasting with competitors lacking this specific validation or those like IBM who have slipped in key categories.
*   Highlight Kore.ai's ABL for deterministic, auditable agent behavior, particularly for regulated industries, as a key differentiator against probabilistic LLM-centric approaches and newer, less battle-tested governance offerings.
*   Emphasize Kore.ai's true omnichannel and native voice capabilities, showcasing how they overcome the limitations of voice-first or ITSM-centric platforms that rely on third-party telephony for comprehensive customer engagement.
