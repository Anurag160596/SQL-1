# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Wednesday, 24 June 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are pushing AI agent capabilities, but often with unproven governance and opaque pricing models. Kore.ai's ABL and proven enterprise platform depth remain critical differentiators against these emerging, yet unvalidated, agentic solutions.

## Three signals that matter

### Five9's Agentic Pivot Under Seat-Model Pressure
**What happened —** (2026-06-23) Five9 launched "Voice AI Agents" to replace IVR and announced a pivot to "Agentic CX," while also reporting 9% revenue growth and significant AI revenue acceleration. ([Business Wire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260623005303/en/Five9-Launches-Breakthrough-New-Release-of-Voice-AI-Agents-to-Power-the-Next-Generation-of-Agentic-Self-Service)), ([No Jitter](https://www.nojitter.com/contact-centers/five9-reported-strong-q1-2026-earnings-ai-growth)).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This highlights the market's shift towards agentic AI, but Five9's reliance on a seat-based model and the inherent risk of AI deflection remains a core vulnerability. Kore.ai's cost-per-outcome, deterministic ABL, and proven enterprise platform breadth offer a more predictable and scalable solution.

### Glean and TinyFish Bridge AI Agents to Live Web Data
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) Glean partnered with TinyFish to enable AI agents to act on live web data within secure enterprise contexts, expanding Glean's capabilities beyond internal knowledge search. ([Pluang](https://www.pluang.com/news/tinyfish-and-glean-partner-to-enable-ai-agents-to-act-on-live-web-data-within-secure-enterprise-contexts))
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This move by Glean, while focused on internal productivity, signals a broader trend of AI agents needing real-time data access. Kore.ai's robust ABL and established integration framework already support secure, governed access to live enterprise data for customer-facing agents, offering a more mature and CX-focused solution.

### Cresta Showcases AI CX Capabilities Amidst Broader Market Push
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) Cresta showcased its AI customer experience capabilities at CCW Las Vegas, emphasizing its AI Analyst and agent-assist features. ([TipRanks](https://www.tipranks.com/news/tipranks-news/cresta-showcases-ai-customer-experience-capabilities-at-ccw-las-vegas))
**What it means for Kore.ai —** Cresta's continued focus on conversation intelligence and agent-assist is a recognized strength, but it underscores their narrower scope compared to Kore.ai's full-lifecycle, omnichannel platform. Kore.ai's ABL provides compile-time validation and deterministic execution, offering superior reliability and governance for complex enterprise CX workflows.

## Watch for
- Continued consolidation and integration efforts by larger players like ServiceNow (with Moveworks) and NICE (with Cognigy).
- Emergence of more specific AI agent capabilities for niche industry verticals from smaller players.
- Evolving pricing models as vendors grapple with consumption-based AI costs and enterprise predictability demands.

## Recommended actions
- **Reinforce Kore.ai's ABL advantage:** Emphasize compile-time validation, deterministic execution, and per-agent cost attribution in all customer conversations, particularly when competitors highlight AI agent capabilities.
- **Highlight analyst leadership:** Leverage Gartner and Forrester's recognition of Kore.ai as a Leader in Conversational AI for Customer Service to counter competitors lacking this independent validation.
- **Arm sales with TCO predictability messaging:** Equip sales teams to articulate how Kore.ai's transparent pricing and cost attribution models mitigate the risks associated with competitor consumption-based AI pricing.
- **Showcase true omnichannel depth:** Continue to differentiate Kore.ai's native voice and digital omnichannel capabilities against voice-first or voice-dominant solutions.
- **Monitor partner integrations:** Track how competitors are stitching together capabilities via partnerships, and position Kore.ai's integrated, single-stack platform as a more robust and reliable enterprise solution.
