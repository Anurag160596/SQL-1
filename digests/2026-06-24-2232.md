**What it means for Kore.ai —** This gap in pricing transparency presents an opportunity for Kore.ai to emphasize its own transparent and predictable pricing model, highlighting the benefits of cost-per-outcome over per-seat models.

### Proof/Validation Gap
**What happened —** (2026-06-23) Parloa, a voice-first contact center AI vendor, lacks independent analyst validation for its platform, despite its funding and customer growth ([Chain Store Age](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMioAFBVV95cUxOTFFBTjJwYjhGd3J0Um5Nc0NmWkVfUThvTGdrYjZBY1p0MUxWeHZuUmdfRnpldXlNR3FFZWFpWUNnRHVNbUFQM2FRTWtMQjdxaDdqQlA0VUktTzAwbjNzbHY1N1Fld2p4SW9RUnN0QU5RQzBBTjBrR2ZMZWFYYjdUYmwtTzBGS081d3E3V2JVM2RseEkyS0NPcG1XN1BGc0Jm)).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** Kore.ai can leverage its strong analyst validation across multiple reports (Gartner, Forrester, Everest, IDC) to contrast with Parloa's lack of recognition, emphasizing the importance of independent validation in the enterprise conversational AI market.

### Reliability Concerns
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) Genesys faced a multi-month outage in its UAE region, highlighting concerns over the reliability of its cloud infrastructure and the impact on its customers ([Genesys](https://status.mypurecloud.com/history)).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This incident underscores the importance of reliability and uptime in contact center operations. Kore.ai can emphasize its own focus on high availability and redundancy, positioning itself as a more reliable option for enterprises requiring continuous customer service operations.
