# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Wednesday, 24 June 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Five9 is aggressively pushing new Voice AI Agents, directly challenging Kore.ai in core contact center automation, while Salesforce's Agentforce continues its horizontal expansion across business functions, potentially diluting its CX focus. These moves create immediate opportunities for Kore.ai to highlight its specialized, analyst-validated leadership in governed, omnichannel conversational AI for CX.

## Three signals that matter

### Five9 Challenges Voice Automation with New Agents
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) Five9 launched new Voice AI Agents designed to replace traditional IVRs, emphasizing human oversight as central to its strategy at CCW (CX Today; Directors Club News; CMSWire).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This is a direct competitive play into high-value voice automation. Kore.ai can undercut Five9's structurally exposed seat-based pricing model and highlight its ABL for deterministic, auditable agent behavior, contrasting with Five9's newer AI governance and implied reliance on "human oversight" for trust.

### Salesforce Agentforce Expands Horizontally Beyond CX
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) Salesforce's Agentforce is broadening its application across retail revenue, marketing workflows, fan engagement (VCARB deployment), and revenue management (Petty Products deployment), signaling a push for horizontal agentic AI across its cloud ecosystem (The Futurum Group; Techzine Global; CX Today; Business Wire).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** Salesforce's wide-ranging focus creates an opening to position Kore.ai as the specialized, analyst-validated Leader in *customer-facing conversational AI*. This contrasts with Salesforce's broader CRM/ITSM platform, which has known challenges with pricing predictability and platform lock-in for dedicated CX use cases.
