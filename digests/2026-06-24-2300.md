# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Wednesday, 24 June 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Five9's launch of Voice AI Agents and Salesforce's Agentforce Commerce push underscore the growing importance of agentic AI in customer experience, but Kore.ai's deterministic approach and cost-per-outcome model position it for advantage in reliability and TCO.

## Three signals that matter
The **three most important** moves this run, each as its own block.

### Five9 Launches Voice AI Agents
**What happened —** (2026-06-23) Five9 launched Voice AI Agents to replace contact center IVR, as reported by CMSWire [CMSWire](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMinAFBVV95cUxPQklSbEhKaDd4VG5yM3pMV0FzX21pdm96dTBYdURCalJkeUN2a0ZNMGE2Y2pNblZEbjIzQXlUM0ZtcS1XbEhsSjBiS1hQVlNZUmY1VVZlUjhqWUthNGxRb25ZTTJUcUdaVHBJUGpST2JuYXN2WUNKRHJOcXVnOFJQcTVwbHYwNmF3ckk2QWhWUE1ZSDVocERBaU5kNEU), indicating a shift towards more automated and intelligent customer service solutions.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This move highlights the increasing adoption of AI in contact centers, but Five9's seat-based model and lack of determinism in its AI agents leave room for Kore.ai to differentiate itself with its cost-per-outcome model and deterministic approach, ensuring more reliable and auditable interactions.

### Salesforce Expands Agentforce
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) Salesforce announced Agentforce Commerce, pushing agentic AI from hype to retail revenue reality, according to The Futurum Group [The Futurum Group](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMivAFBVV95cUxQZFZwNmJ0THhfM0ZZVTNiWmQteVhzYWFobUxoX08wZ29PQS05bFpuU2FuNG4xRnhoX09GMXdSNmpmVjgwRDZFWjl4YnRONE96SEkxdGFMcVVINUExUXAyMExmSFZqejFCR1BreDhUQm1ST2tEVDZFRHJMcWs1dVotUG1tRDZjeEFVdXVZQmFlRWJyNjRHczdUcW5xSFAwbzdSZ1czRWZiVmx6Q0RqMktOMk1odmtYY25BNjVPdQ), signaling a significant expansion of its agentic AI capabilities into commerce and customer experience.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While Salesforce's move into commerce with Agentforce Commerce is notable, its pricing model and consumption-based costs may lead to unpredictability for customers, allowing Kore.ai to emphasize its transparent and cost-effective approach to enterprise AI solutions, leveraging its compile-time validation and deterministic execution.

### IBM Partners with ElevenLabs
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) IBM partnered with ElevenLabs, as reported by AI Magazine [AI Magazine](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiggFBVV95cUxPUjFzX2IzQl90NFJVclRXT1piZURkXzQwRkhGSzZmdmdYZ2RDaVRUd0dDWUJXdi1hTTFJajQ2ZkZwbm9pWGZtR0s2RnJsc2xia0ktWXFjSkU3WE0zSGdIUWZGRGxTZDVuMERhaHFHSHB0bXN2THlfSmhCenR6T25wZVpR), to enhance its enterprise AI capabilities, particularly in voice and conversational AI, indicating a strategic move to strengthen its position in the market.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This partnership may enhance IBM's capabilities, but its complexity and the potential for overlapping technologies could create integration challenges, providing an opportunity for Kore.ai to highlight its streamlined, single-stack approach to enterprise AI, offering a more cohesive and manageable solution for customers.
