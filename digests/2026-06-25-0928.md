# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Thursday, 25 June 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are aggressively pushing new agentic AI capabilities, particularly in voice and specialized enterprise workflows, but often with underlying weaknesses in pricing predictability, true omnichannel integration, or independent CX analyst validation. Kore.ai can leverage its ABL's deterministic governance, transparent cost model, and proven Leader status in conversational AI for CX to undercut these moves.

## Three signals that matter

## Watch for
*   Five9's Voice AI agent adoption rates and production containment metrics, especially for complex or regulated flows.
*   Salesforce Agentforce Commerce customer case studies and any further changes or clarifications to their "Flex Credits" pricing model.

## Recommended actions
*   Reinforce Kore.ai's ABL for deterministic, auditable agent behavior, especially for regulated industries, contrasting with probabilistic LLM agents or bolt-on AI.
*   Emphasize Kore.ai's transparent, predictable cost-per-outcome model against competitors' consumption-based or seat-based pricing with overage risks.
*   Highlight Kore.ai's unified, true omnichannel platform and Leader status in CX-specific conversational AI MQs against CCaaS incumbents' bolt-on AI and big-tech's platform lock-in.
