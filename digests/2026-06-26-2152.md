# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Friday, 26 June 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are intensifying efforts on AI agent pricing models and deployment speed (Agentforce) while simultaneously highlighting the need for human oversight (Five9) or grappling with negative public perception of basic chatbots (Parloa). This creates an opening for Kore.ai to emphasize its differentiated, governed, and truly agentic platform that delivers reliable, trusted automation and predictable TCO.

## Three signals that matter

### Agentforce pushes resolution-based pricing and rapid deployment
**What happened —** (2026-06-25) Salesforce launched an Agentforce Help Agent that deploys in minutes and only charges for resolutions, alongside expanding Agentforce into Salesforce Suites and Slack CRM for small businesses in the Philippines (2026-06-26) — [Salesforce](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMigAFBVV95cUxPZVRVaWpYRzZzWGVzZlJXOS1TYWcyUTU4QkZFNHQ2ZTRVd2hpcGd2VGhIUVZvdXJTTTVxS1JvOFNsTDI4eC1md1hUUklVMjRFcGRBbkUyN0tMcGJjMEZDYTdQdkpESGlfc18ySjV1TGhUVXV4NmFVcmRxa1ZyZnF0Nw?oc=5) and Salesforce.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This highlights a shift towards outcome-based pricing and rapid deployment, but Agentforce's history of "pricing chaos" and "credit anxiety" (KB) creates an opening for Kore.ai to emphasize its predictable, per-agent/intent/tool cost attribution and ABL's deterministic execution for *reliable*, auditable resolutions, avoiding ambiguity around "what counts as a resolution."

### Parloa survey reveals widespread chatbot avoidance
**What happened —** (2026-06-25) A Parloa-commissioned survey found that over half of Americans actively avoid chatbots, reflecting ongoing consumer skepticism towards conversational AI — NewsBytes.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This validates Kore.ai's focus on delivering sophisticated, truly agentic AI with ABL's compile-time validation and deterministic execution to build trust and deliver effective, human-like interactions, directly countering the negative perception associated with basic, frustrating chatbots.

### Five9 emphasizes human oversight in AI strategy
**What happened —** (2026-06-24) Five9 announced that human oversight is central to its new Voice AI Strategy at CCW, supported by new research showing 92% AI adoption in CX but continued consumer reliance on human support — CX Today and Business Wire.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While human oversight is always important, Five9's strong emphasis suggests their AI agents may lack the maturity and determinism for truly autonomous, trusted interactions, exposing their "bolt-on" AI (KB). Kore.ai's ABL-driven agents are designed for high reliability and auditability, reducing the *need* for constant human intervention in routine tasks and building inherent trust.
