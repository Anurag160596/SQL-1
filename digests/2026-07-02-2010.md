# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Thursday, 02 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Salesforce's aggressive move to performance-based pricing for its Agentforce Help Agent directly challenges Kore.ai's outcome-driven value proposition, while Genesys and ServiceNow continue to expand their AI capabilities through acquisitions and partnerships, signaling a rapidly evolving market for agentic AI.

## Three signals that matter

### Agentforce Shifts to Performance-Based Pricing
**What happened —** (2026-07-02) Salesforce unveiled its Agentforce Help Agent with performance-based pricing, charging only for resolved support cases, a model specifically highlighted in its launch in Korea. 디지털투데이, Chosunbiz
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This is a direct competitive move into outcome-based pricing, a core Kore.ai strength. Kore.ai should emphasize its proven, transparent cost attribution per agent/intent/tool and platform-agnostic approach, contrasting with Salesforce's history of pricing changes and Data Cloud lock-in.

## Watch for
*   Further details on Salesforce's performance-based pricing model and its impact on customer adoption and TCO.
*   Genesys's integration of Pinkfish and the launch of new agentic AI capabilities.
*   ServiceNow's continued expansion into non-ITSM areas via acquisitions and partnerships.
*   Five9's strategic revamp and any further executive or organizational changes.

## Recommended actions
*   Arm Sales with battlecards emphasizing Kore.ai's transparent, auditable cost-per-outcome model against Salesforce's new pricing.
*   Highlight Kore.ai's unified, purpose-built agentic platform against CCaaS incumbents' bolt-on AI and big-tech's ITSM/CRM heritage.
*   Emphasize Kore.ai's platform stability and consistent roadmap to capitalize on Five9's ongoing organizational shifts.
*   Brief Analyst Relations on Kore.ai's ABL and deterministic execution as a key differentiator for governance and reliability, especially as competitors acquire or partner to accelerate AI.
