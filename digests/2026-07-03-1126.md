# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Friday, 03 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are aggressively expanding AI agent capabilities and integrating them into existing platforms, but significant gaps remain in demonstrable enterprise-grade governance, true omnichannel depth, and predictable TCO, which Kore.ai's ABL and unified platform directly address.

## Three signals that matter

### Genesys Accelerates Agentic AI with Pinkfish Acquisition
**What happened —** Genesys acquires Pinkfish to speed up contact center AI deployments, as reported by MarketScale.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This acquisition signals Genesys's continued focus on bolstering its AI agent capabilities. Kore.ai's ABL offers a more robust, compile-time validated approach to agent development, mitigating the risks of production failures inherent in less structured acquisition-driven AI stacks.

### ServiceNow Deepens Agentic AI with ai.work Acquisition and Accenture Partnership
**What happened —** ServiceNow acquired ai.work and partnered with Accenture to accelerate the shift to agentic AI, as detailed by CTech and WebWire.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** ServiceNow is doubling down on agentic AI, aiming to integrate it across its platform. Kore.ai's established, unified platform and proven analyst leadership in conversational AI for CX provide a more mature and less fragmented alternative to ServiceNow's piecemeal acquisitions and integrations.

### IBM Positions watsonx Orchestrate as an Agentic Control Plane
**What happened —** IBM announced its agentic control plane for watsonx Orchestrate, allowing control over multiple AI agents, as reported by IBM.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** IBM is emphasizing a multi-vendor approach to agent management. Kore.ai's platform-native ABL provides deterministic control and compile-time validation for agent behavior, offering a more reliable and integrated solution than a multi-vendor control plane, which can introduce complexity and governance challenges.
