# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Friday, 03 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors like Genesys and ServiceNow are accelerating their agentic AI plays through acquisitions and platform integration, but their bolted-on approaches and complex pricing models present clear openings for Kore.ai's unified, deterministic, and transparently priced platform.

## Three signals that matter

### Genesys Acquires Pinkfish for Agentic AI Acceleration
**What happened —** (2026-07-01) Genesys acquires Pinkfish to accelerate agentic AI in contact centers. MarketScale
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This acquisition highlights Genesys's strategy of bolting on AI capabilities rather than building them natively, creating potential integration complexities and roadmap ambiguities that Kore.ai's unified platform avoids. Our ABL's compile-time validation and deterministic execution offer superior reliability for enterprise CX compared to stitched-together solutions.

### ServiceNow Deepens Agentic AI with ai.work Acquisition
**What happened —** (2026-07-02) ServiceNow acquires ai.work for tens of millions, deepening Israel buying spree. CTech
**What it means for Kore.ai —** ServiceNow's continued investment in agentic AI, now through acquisition, underscores the market's demand for robust AI capabilities. However, their ITSM-centric heritage and reliance on third-party voice solutions present an opportunity for Kore.ai to emphasize its purpose-built, omnichannel CX platform and its native voice capabilities, backed by leading analyst validation.

### IBM Positions watsonx Orchestrate as Agentic Control Plane
**What happened —** (2026-07-02) Agentic Control Plane in IBM watsonx Orchestrate: One place to control every AI agent. IBM
**What it means for Kore.ai —** IBM's shift to a "control plane" strategy, while acknowledging the need for agent orchestration, highlights the complexity of managing multiple AI models and agents. Kore.ai's ABL provides a unified, deterministic framework for agent development and execution, offering greater transparency and control than a multi-vendor orchestration layer, especially for regulated CX workflows.
