# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Saturday, 04 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are actively acquiring and repositioning their agentic AI offerings, with Genesys and ServiceNow making direct acquisitions to accelerate capabilities, while IBM and Cresta are refining their narratives around agent orchestration and testing. This signals a market consolidating around agentic capabilities and the need for robust control and validation, an area where Kore.ai's ABL offers a significant structural advantage over bolt-on or prompt-based approaches.

## Three signals that matter

### Genesys and ServiceNow acquire for agentic AI acceleration
**What happened —** Genesys acquired Pinkfish to accelerate agentic AI in contact centers (2026-07-01) [MarketScale]. Separately, ServiceNow acquired ai.work for tens of millions, deepening its Israel buying spree (2026-07-02) [CTech], following its recent broad AI deployment across major business functions (2026-07-02) [MarketScale].
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This highlights a clear trend of incumbents buying rather than purely building agentic capabilities, which can lead to integration challenges and fragmented roadmaps. Kore.ai's unified, purpose-built platform with ABL offers superior integration stability and governance compared to bolt-on acquisitions.

### IBM and Cresta push agentic control and testing narratives
**What happened —** IBM positioned its watsonx Orchestrate as an "Agentic Control Plane: One place to control every AI agent" (2026-07-02) [IBM]. Concurrently, Cresta's CMO discussed the "Unified AI Workforce" and why "Synthetic Customers Are the Next Big Thing" (2026-07-02) [CX Today].
**What it means for Kore.ai —** These narratives validate the market's need for robust agent management and testing. Kore.ai's ABL provides a true agentic control plane with compile-time validation and deterministic execution, offering a more reliable and auditable approach than prompt-plus-code methods, while synthetic testing complements ABL's structural integrity checks.

### NICE and Five9 focus on AI enablement and platform revamp
**What happened —** NiCE launched an AI Specialization Program to recognize partners driving significant AI outcomes for enterprises (2026-07-01) [01net]. Separately, Constellation Research published a report on Five9's "revamp and where it's headed" (2026-07-01) [Constellation Research], indicating a strategic pivot likely driven by AI pressures.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While competitors evolve their ecosystems and platforms, Kore.ai maintains a strong lead as a Leader in CX-specific conversational AI evaluations. This is an opportunity to highlight Kore.ai's mature, proven AI capabilities and predictable TCO, contrasting with Five9's organizational instability and analyst gaps, and NICE's ongoing Cognigy integration.

## Recommended actions

*   Reinforce messaging around Kore.ai's unified, purpose-built agentic AI platform, contrasting it with competitors' bolt-on acquisitions and fragmented AI lineages.
*   Proactively position Kore.ai's ABL as the industry's most robust "agentic control plane," emphasizing compile-time validation, deterministic execution, and superior governance over prompt-based or loosely integrated approaches.
*   Arm sales and product marketing with specific proof points on Kore.ai's analyst leadership in CX-specific conversational AI, especially against CCaaS incumbents like Five9 and big-tech players like IBM and Salesforce.
