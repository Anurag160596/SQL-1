# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Saturday, 04 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are aggressively packaging their AI capabilities, with ServiceNow leading the charge by unifying its agentic offerings under the "Otto" brand and acquiring Moveworks. This highlights a market trend towards integrated agentic platforms, underscoring Kore.ai's advantage in providing a single, purpose-built, and analyst-validated platform for CX automation.

## Three signals that matter

### ServiceNow Unifies Agentic AI Under "Otto" Brand
**What happened —** ServiceNow announced the unification of its AI agent capabilities, including the recent acquisition of Moveworks, under the new "Otto" brand, aiming to deliver AI across every major business function. This move integrates AI agents, Now Assist, and AI Agent Studio into a cohesive offering. ### ServiceNow & Accenture Partner on Agentic AI Transformation
**What happened —** ServiceNow and Accenture have launched new AI-powered services designed to accelerate the shift from legacy risk platforms to agentic AI, focusing on enterprise-wide transformation. (2026-07-02) [WebWire](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiYEFVX3lxTE95RkxmdE0ySEhWY2dVY2l3NWctYllDMDFUMVRwcnQxeXRkMlVuVGNPdE1uczF1TXB6bkIyNWhibG9GNml6QUEteng4bGMwb1BzTy1veXNRXzNHTTZZeFpWaA?oc=5)

Kore.ai's proven platform depth and analyst validation in CX offer a more direct and purpose-built path for enterprises seeking agentic AI for customer experience.

### IBM Positions watsonx Orchestrate as Agentic Control Plane
**What happened —** IBM is repositioning its watsonx Orchestrate platform as an "agentic control plane," emphasizing its ability to manage AI agents from various vendors, including third-party LLMs like Granite, Claude, GPT, and Mistral. This move aims to provide a unified interface for controlling diverse AI agents. **What it means for Kore.ai —** IBM's strategy acknowledges the complexity of managing multiple AI models and agents. Kore.ai's ABL and multi-model routing capabilities offer a more robust, compile-time validated approach to agent orchestration and model management, ensuring deterministic execution and cost control, rather than relying on a "control plane" layer over potentially unmanaged agents.

- Continued integration of Moveworks into ServiceNow's Otto platform and its impact on enterprise adoption.
- Any further analyst evaluations that position ServiceNow's CX capabilities relative to pure-play conversational AI leaders.
- IBM's progress in demonstrating the efficacy of its "agentic control plane" for customer-facing use cases.

## Recommended actions
- Emphasize Kore.ai's unified, purpose-built CX platform and its analyst-validated leadership in Conversational AI for Customer Service.
- Highlight the risks associated with ITSM-centric platforms expanding into CX, particularly regarding integration complexity and potential for fragmented AI strategies.
- Reinforce the value of ABL's compile-time validation and deterministic execution for enterprise-grade CX automation, contrasting it with less robust "control plane" approaches.
- Leverage the ServiceNow "Otto" announcement to position Kore.ai as the established, specialized leader for comprehensive CX agentic AI.
