# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Sunday, 05 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** ServiceNow is aggressively expanding its agentic AI footprint through acquisitions, partnerships, and ambitious revenue targets, signaling a strong push into enterprise AI that Kore.ai must counter with its specialized CX focus and proven governance.

## Three signals that matter

### ServiceNow's Aggressive AI Expansion
**What happened —** (2026-07-04) ServiceNow announced an AI revenue target of $1.5B as a large investor builds a stake, following its (2026-07-02) acquisition of ai.work and a new partnership with Accenture to launch AI-powered services for agentic AI shifts AD HOC NEWS, CTech, and WebWire.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** ServiceNow's aggressive M&A and partnerships signal a broad enterprise AI play, but Kore.ai can highlight its purpose-built CX focus and analyst-validated leadership in conversational AI, contrasting with ServiceNow's ITSM heritage and "assists" consumption model unpredictability.

### ServiceNow's Pervasive AI Deployment
**What happened —** (2026-07-02) ServiceNow announced the deployment of AI across every major business function, urging enterprise leaders to evaluate before following suit MarketScale.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While ServiceNow pushes broad AI adoption, Kore.ai can emphasize its deep, specialized agentic AI platform for CX, offering compile-time validation and deterministic execution crucial for high-stakes customer interactions, unlike a generic "every function" approach.

### Cresta's Vision for Synthetic Customers
**What happened —** (2026-07-02) Cresta's CMO discussed the "Unified AI Workforce" and positioned "Synthetic Customers" as the next big thing, highlighting their strategy for AI in CX CX Today.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** Cresta's focus on synthetic customers for testing aligns with its conversation-data moat, but Kore.ai can counter by emphasizing its ABL's compile-time validation and Git-native release engineering for robust, deterministic agent development, reducing reliance on post-build testing.
