# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Monday, 06 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Agentforce's new outcome-based pricing model, coupled with recent layoffs, signals potential instability and a shift in strategy that could introduce new complexities for customers. Meanwhile, Sierra's high valuation continues to outpace its proven CX analyst validation, reinforcing Kore.ai's position as a stable, validated enterprise leader.

## Three signals that matter

### Agentforce shifts to outcome-based pricing, raising predictability questions
**What happened —** (2026-07-05) Salesforce announced it will now charge for Agentforce AI agents only when they successfully resolve customer issues, a shift from previous consumption models [thelec.net](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiZ0FVX3lxTE5EcjIwR0pTV2tDQ3RyWXhjZlZBcW9JVWR5alpPZ2NTbzNRN1RYdkhhQjJ5eE12MmFOc2k3RUhnQVpGZWNNNjdCVzFjZnB6NEFLNHpVWWJadTM4SkdVdEc2UTMxOVJkd00?oc=5).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While seemingly customer-friendly, this "outcome-based" pricing can introduce ambiguity around what constitutes a "successful resolution" and lead to unpredictable costs, a known concern with Agentforce's prior pricing chaos. Kore.ai offers clear, auditable cost attribution per agent/intent/tool, coupled with deterministic execution for predictable outcomes and transparent TCO.

### Agentforce unit hit by Salesforce layoffs, signaling potential instability
**What happened —** (2026-07-04) Salesforce conducted layoffs that impacted its Agentforce, MuleSoft, and Marketing Cloud units [qz.com](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiggFBVV95cUxOdnlDaHdYeWtGY0RCMzU5UWRIQmNpM200MmhPVmZjTktOLTBXZEFUNER4Sm9GREF2ZjhzZ1F6c25wc184Wl81Tks3SVhkVEFPZktXWnRTX1g4NG5fcENpcHE4M0hMai0tVXRuSE5sSFN1VW1aclBZZFJuZy13OXNGRE53?oc=5).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** Layoffs within a key AI initiative like Agentforce signal potential organizational instability and roadmap uncertainty, which can concern enterprise buyers making multi-year platform investments. Kore.ai offers a stable, focused, and analyst-validated platform for mission-critical enterprise CX automation.

### Sierra's valuation continues to outpace proven CX analyst validation
**What happened —** (2026-07-03) Sierra reported $200M ARR in 2026 against a $15.8B valuation, indicating a significant revenue-to-valuation gap [GetLatka](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiTkFVX3lxTE1jWjZ1X255MG9acm1fMU0wdEZKa0t1TDJURVNvUlpQX2Q3Tk4xZTVqVElUTmU2V0hqdkJHUmwySVRxWXZqVVpTcWFxcmQtUQ?oc=5).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This reinforces the narrative that Sierra's market hype and funding outrun its independent CX analyst validation, particularly where Kore.ai is a Leader in both Gartner and Forrester for Conversational AI for Customer Service.

## Watch for
*   Further details on Agentforce's "successful resolution" definition and its impact on customer TCO.
*   Any additional organizational changes or executive departures at Salesforce's Agentforce unit.
*   Sierra's pursuit of independent CX analyst validation to match its high valuation.

## Recommended actions
*   Arm sales with clear messaging on Agentforce's new pricing model, highlighting potential ambiguities and contrasting it with Kore.ai's predictable, auditable cost attribution.
*   Emphasize Kore.ai's organizational stability and focused commitment to enterprise CX automation in competitive deals against Agentforce.
*   Reinforce Kore.ai's strong analyst validation (Gartner, Forrester Leader) as a counter to Sierra's high valuation-to-revenue ratio and lack of specific CX leadership recognition.
*   Continue to position Kore.ai's true omnichannel capabilities against voice-first vendors like PolyAI, even as they show growth.
