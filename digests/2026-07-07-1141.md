# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Tuesday, 07 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Salesforce's Agentforce is aggressively addressing TCO concerns with new outcome-based pricing while broadening its ecosystem integration, directly challenging Kore.ai's value proposition. Meanwhile, Genesys is attempting to fill CX gaps via acquisition, reinforcing Kore.ai's advantage as a unified, AI-native platform.

## Three signals that matter

### Agentforce shifts to outcome-based pricing
**What happened —** Salesforce announced it will only charge for Agentforce AI agents that successfully resolve customer issues, a significant move to align costs with value and address TCO concerns, as reported by [thelec.net](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiZ0FVX3lxTE5EcjIwR0pTV2tDQ3RyWXhjZlZBcW9JVWR5alpPZ2NTbzNRN1RYdkhhQjJ5eE12MmFOc2k3RUhnQVpGZWNNNjdCVzFjZnB6NEFLNHpVWWJadTM4SkdVdEc2UTMxOVJkd00?oc=5).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This directly targets a known Agentforce weakness (pricing unpredictability) and a common CIO concern. Kore.ai can counter by emphasizing its Agent Behavior Language (ABL)'s deterministic execution and transparent, per-agent/intent/tool cost attribution, which provides predictable TCO even as automation scales, without relying on subjective "successful resolution" definitions.

### Agentforce deepens ecosystem integration
**What happened —** Salesforce is significantly broadening its Agentforce AI rollout, integrating agents as "teammates" in Slack and launching its biggest Agentforce Commerce release yet, as announced by [Salesforce](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMi7wFBVV95cUxQQ21CcHN1YzktVXE1a1RGTWFIY2hzTXVLZzh3amszYmFTYnlzQ2dLcGVHTnpwd2dVVXN2dlozbndPRW5SM0dYeUJCNHgyNWoyYWlDTXltQTFhSi1uMExscVVQaUpsb1NiN1p6dDAyLVBrUGo0d3VZSUt3Y2ptWnNmMGRwX2t1UHhpMVlxOGw4c3VMal9xemFfa3pWeUhjbkVBNGViRXI3OGZCZkxHclY5VjVpdDk2d2ZzeFIwcW0wOXYyejBNQUZsWWdqaFRRYU9NU0F0RUowV3diUU9kTFFycjROc2tEczR4Y1JGQlhWdw?oc=5) and [Tech in Asia](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMifkFVX3lxTE9USS1yMlVqZ21UUXloWGFUck1lMGtMb2s2U19qX2dGWmtDcmRDQzctMEI2RkRFOWtUQXdXOG1XdWJZTXFCa3VtWjg0VkdMMk5yVlp5eDlxSUVtOU56S2JMUmI5LUFmdTRJQ045cjdLV1M2R05pc00tNVhBU1R6Zw?oc=5).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This reinforces Salesforce's strategy of leveraging its vast CRM ecosystem. Kore.ai must continue to highlight its pure-play leadership in *conversational AI for CX* (Gartner/Forrester Leader) and its agnostic, full-scope omnichannel platform that integrates deeply with *any* CRM/ERP, not just Salesforce, offering greater flexibility and avoiding platform lock-in.

### Genesys acquires to bridge CX gaps
**What happened —** Genesys acquired Pinkfish, aiming to bridge a longstanding CX gap within its platform, signaling an ongoing effort to enhance capabilities through M&A, according to [No Jitter](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMipwFBVV95cUxOTXZIa0M2SHhlZV9BS0llOUVtRjlibHh0cE9jcnlab21BRGNmTjJIaG5rSHZHeENjcTZEU3VjZ2hlckU5ZmhRdUVjTTJJbVNodll2a3BnX1A4dGtYR0NtQTE2c2p0dGFCQ3JmYTRkeTBPMFZOOGpJdDRPTGprRlQ1dnBaMVNtN1ZfTVZhNy1jZWhNem1nbkdkY3k2V2I0TVNWMkpvQk13Zw?oc=5).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This is an opportunity to position Kore.ai's unified, AI-native agentic platform as inherently more robust and integrated than CCaaS incumbents that rely on bolt-on acquisitions to fill capability gaps. Kore.ai's single stack across voice and digital, with native voice capabilities, offers a more cohesive and reliable solution.

## Watch for
*   Further details on how Agentforce defines "successful resolution" for its new pricing model and its impact on customer TCO.
*   Specifics on the CX gaps Genesys aims to bridge with the Pinkfish acquisition and the integration roadmap.
*   Any impact of the Agentforce layoffs on product roadmap or customer support.

## Recommended actions
*   Arm sales with messaging on Kore.ai's deterministic cost attribution and compile-time validation (ABL) as a superior alternative to consumption models based on subjective "successful resolutions."
*   Reinforce Kore.ai's position as a pure-play, analyst-validated Leader in *conversational AI for CX*, emphasizing its omnichannel breadth and agnostic integration capabilities against platform lock-in.
*   Highlight Kore.ai's unified, AI-native platform advantage over CCaaS incumbents that rely on bolt-on acquisitions to address core CX gaps.
*   Engage Analyst Relations to proactively position Kore.ai's transparent TCO and platform stability in upcoming analyst briefings.
