# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Tuesday, 07 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are aggressively pushing AI agent capabilities, with Salesforce launching significant updates to Agentforce and Genesys highlighting its AI investments. However, these moves often highlight platform lock-in and potential cost unpredictability, areas where Kore.ai's ABL-driven deterministic execution and transparent pricing offer a distinct advantage.

### Salesforce Agentforce Accelerates Agentic Capabilities and Integration
**What happened —** (2026-07-07) Salesforce announced significant advancements for Agentforce, integrating AI agents into Slack as "teammates," detailing how enterprises can leverage Agentforce for global sales success, and emphasizing the need for new strategies in the "Agentforce Operations era." They also launched their "biggest Agentforce Commerce Release Yet" as AI agents transform commerce. (2026-07-04) Salesforce also saw layoffs impacting Agentforce, MuleSoft, and Marketing Cloud.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** Salesforce's rapid iteration on Agentforce, while impressive, underscores the complexity of integrating AI agents across their ecosystem and the potential for platform lock-in. Kore.ai's ABL provides a vendor-agnostic approach with compile-time validation, offering greater flexibility and predictability compared to Salesforce's evolving, platform-dependent model.

### Genesys Acquires Pinkfish to Bridge CX Gap with AI
**What happened —** (2026-07-06) Genesys announced its acquisition of Pinkfish, aiming to bridge a longstanding CX gap with AI capabilities.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This acquisition signals Genesys's ongoing efforts to bolster its AI offerings, but it also points to a strategy of acquiring niche capabilities rather than building a unified, native AI platform. Kore.ai's integrated, single-stack approach for voice and digital AI, coupled with its analyst-validated leadership in Conversational AI, offers a more cohesive and robust solution.

### Five9 Automates Dealership Operations with AI
**What happened —** (2026-07-05) Five9 is enabling dealerships to automate calls, service scheduling, and F&I training through its AI capabilities.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While Five9 demonstrates AI's application in specific verticals, its core business remains heavily seat-based, creating inherent TCO risks as AI deflection increases. Kore.ai's outcome-based pricing and deterministic AI offer a more predictable and scalable solution for enterprises looking to achieve true automation without the overage anxieties of consumption-based models.

## Watch for
- Continued integration and messaging around Salesforce's Agentforce 3.0+ and its impact on their pricing and platform lock-in.
- Further details on Genesys's Pinkfish integration and its impact on their native AI strategy.
- Any signs of broader enterprise adoption or specific use cases for Five9's AI in the automotive sector.

## Recommended actions
- Emphasize Kore.ai's ABL in all sales and marketing collateral, highlighting compile-time validation and deterministic execution as key differentiators against complex, evolving platforms like Salesforce Agentforce.
- Reinforce Kore.ai's analyst leadership in Conversational AI for Customer Service (Forrester Wave, Gartner MQ) as a counterpoint to CCaaS incumbents like Genesys and Five9 who are acquiring AI capabilities.
- Develop specific messaging around the TCO predictability of Kore.ai's platform, contrasting it with the potential for cost overruns and platform lock-in associated with consumption-based or ecosystem-dependent AI solutions.
- Arm sales teams with clear questions to probe customer concerns around platform integration, cost predictability, and vendor lock-in when evaluating Salesforce Agentforce.
