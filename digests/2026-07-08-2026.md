# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Wednesday, 08 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are doubling down on agentic AI capabilities, with Salesforce and ServiceNow making significant platform plays. While Salesforce's Agentforce is pushing aggressive ARR growth and new features, its complex pricing and reliance on third-party telephony present clear openings for Kore.ai's predictable, integrated, and analyst-validated platform. ServiceNow's integration of Moveworks into its Otto platform highlights its ITSM-centric approach, reinforcing Kore.ai's advantage in true, voice-native omnichannel CX.

## Three signals that matter
### Salesforce Agentforce Pushes Unified Commerce AI with New Release
**What happened —** (2026-07-08) Salesforce unveiled its AI commerce platform, connecting shoppers, merchants, and AI agents, alongside an acquisition of m3ter to expand Agentforce revenue management capabilities FutureCIO, Global Legal Chronicle.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This aggressive push into commerce AI, coupled with a focus on revenue management, highlights Salesforce's ambition but also its platform lock-in. Kore.ai can counter by emphasizing its provider-agnostic model and predictable, per-resolution pricing, contrasting with Salesforce's complex credit-based system and reliance on third-party telephony for voice.

### ServiceNow Integrates Hexnode UEM, Reinforcing ITSM-Centric Agentic AI
**What happened —** (2026-07-08) Hexnode UEM integrates With ServiceNow, Streamlining IT Incident Management and Enhancing Service Efficiency AiThority.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** ServiceNow's continued focus on ITSM and IT operations, even with the Moveworks acquisition, reinforces its core strength in internal workflows rather than external customer-facing voice and omnichannel CX. Kore.ai can highlight its purpose-built, voice-native CX platform and its proven leadership in analyst evaluations for conversational AI in customer service, differentiating from ServiceNow's IT-centric heritage.

### Cresta Joins Telus AI Consortium for Shared Infrastructure
**What happened —** (2026-07-08) Telus joins AI Consortium to develop shared enterprise AI control infrastructure Telecompaper.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While Cresta's participation in an infrastructure consortium signals a focus on broader AI governance, it also highlights their reliance on external frameworks for core control. Kore.ai's Agent Behavior Language (ABL) offers built-in compile-time validation and deterministic execution, providing a more robust and integrated approach to governance and reliability directly within the platform, rather than through consortium participation.
