# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Saturday, 11 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are doubling down on agentic AI capabilities, with a strong emphasis on governance and deployment challenges. While IBM highlights skills across its agentic universe and Cresta launches a training simulator, the core challenge remains bridging the gap between AI potential and reliable enterprise deployment, an area where Kore.ai's ABL and compile-time validation offer a distinct advantage.

## Watch for
- Continued emphasis from competitors on "governance" and "visibility" in agentic AI.
- Any signs of Cresta expanding its AI agent capabilities beyond training into direct automation.
- Further details on IBM's "agentic control plane" and its integration with third-party models.

## Recommended actions
- Arm Sales with messaging that contrasts Kore.ai's ABL-driven compile-time validation and deterministic execution against the probabilistic nature of LLM-based governance solutions from IBM and others.
- Position Kore.ai's direct automation ROI against competitors like Cresta who are focusing on augmenting human agents, emphasizing deflection and efficiency gains.
- Reinforce the narrative that Kore.ai's platform design inherently addresses the "hard part of enterprise AI deployment" that Genesys and others acknowledge, by highlighting our end-to-end platform robustness and ease of integration.
