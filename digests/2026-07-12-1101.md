# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Sunday, 12 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors like ServiceNow and Sierra are leveraging significant funding and platform breadth to gain market traction, but gaps in demonstrable CX-specific analyst validation and predictable TCO for agentic AI remain exploitable weaknesses against Kore.ai's proven, purpose-built platform.

**What it means for Kore.ai —** This signals a potential weakness in ServiceNow's ability to meet specific enterprise AI agent needs, pushing customers towards more specialized or in-house solutions. Kore.ai can highlight its platform-agnostic model approach and robust ABL for building tailored, reliable agents that don't require vendor lock-in or a complete platform overhaul.

## Watch for
- Continued moves by ServiceNow to integrate acquired capabilities (like Moveworks) into their Otto platform, and how this impacts their CX strategy.
- Any shifts in Sierra's narrative or product roadmap to address the CX-specific analyst validation gap.
- Further evidence of "botsitting" or AI budget re-evaluation impacting companies like Glean that focus on internal productivity.

## Recommended actions
- Emphasize Kore.ai's proven leadership in independent CX conversational AI evaluations (Gartner, Forrester) in all sales and marketing collateral.
- Highlight the limitations of platform-centric AI (like ServiceNow's) that require significant customization or vendor lock-in, contrasting with Kore.ai's flexible, model-agnostic approach.
- Position Kore.ai as the solution for predictable TCO and demonstrable ROI in agentic AI for CX, directly countering the potential for "botsitting" and budget overruns seen with internal productivity tools.
- Arm Sales with questions that probe competitors' CX-specific analyst validation and their ability to deliver true omnichannel voice and digital experiences.
