# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Wednesday, 15 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** NICE (Cognigy) secured a significant enterprise banking client for its agentic AI, underscoring the fierce competition for major CX deployments, while Sierra expanded its global reach through a key distribution partnership.

## Three signals that matter

### NICE (Cognigy) wins major banking client for agentic AI
**What happened —** (2026-07-15) Banco do Brasil deployed NiCE Copilot agentic AI to enhance relationship banking and customer service, with further reports detailing the transformation of core banking workflows using NICE Agentic AI Intelligent CIO.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This is a strong enterprise win for a peer-Leader in a regulated industry, validating their agentic AI. Kore.ai must emphasize its unified, purpose-built platform and ABL's compile-time governance for deterministic, auditable behavior, especially for critical banking workflows, contrasting with NICE's bolt-on Cognigy.

### Sierra expands global distribution with SoftBank
**What happened —** (2026-07-14) SoftBank became Sierra's exclusive partner for its AI customer service platform, signing a pact to be the exclusive Japan distributor for the Sierra AI Platform Telecompaper.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** Sierra, a well-funded startup, is actively expanding its market reach beyond its core regions. Kore.ai should continue to highlight its established global enterprise deployments and superior independent CX analyst validation as a Leader in the relevant Gartner and Forrester reports, where Sierra is absent.

### ServiceNow lifts AI revenue target amid stock pressure
**What happened —** (2026-07-14) ServiceNow lifted its AI revenue target to $1.5 billion, signaling strong internal growth projections, though its stock remains mired below analyst price targets Ad-hoc-news.de.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While ServiceNow projects significant AI revenue, its ITSM-centric heritage and "assists" consumption model present TCO unpredictability for CX. Kore.ai can differentiate with its purpose-built external CX focus, native voice, and transparent, predictable cost-per-outcome models.

## Watch for
*   NICE (Cognigy) to announce further enterprise customer wins, particularly in regulated industries, reinforcing their combined AI stack.
*   Sierra's traction in the Japanese market via SoftBank, and any subsequent moves into other geographies.
*   ServiceNow's next earnings call for updates on AI revenue contribution and any further details on "assists" consumption model.
*   Salesforce's Agentforce to clarify pricing models or announce new enterprise CX logos.

## Recommended actions
*   Arm sales with battlecards emphasizing Kore.ai's ABL for deterministic, auditable agent behavior and compile-time validation, especially for regulated industries like banking, directly countering NICE's recent win.
*   Product marketing should reinforce Kore.ai's undisputed leadership in CX-specific conversational AI analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester) against the hype and funding of newer entrants like Sierra.
*   Develop messaging that highlights Kore.ai's predictable TCO and transparent cost-per-outcome for high-volume CX, contrasting with the consumption-based models of big-tech platforms like ServiceNow and Salesforce.
*   Engage analyst relations to proactively brief on Kore.ai's global enterprise traction and unified omnichannel capabilities to reinforce market leadership.
