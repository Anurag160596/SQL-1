# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Tuesday, 21 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Glean is deepening its presence in enterprise agentic workflows through new partnerships, while Salesforce is expanding Agentforce's reach into SMBs and specific verticals like healthcare. These moves highlight the increasing demand for agentic AI across the enterprise, but also expose the need for specialized, governed, and cost-predictable solutions for customer-facing applications, where Kore.ai's ABL and CX-specific analyst leadership provide a distinct advantage.

## Three signals that matter

### Glean expands agentic workflow partnerships, highlights AI error costs
**What happened —** (2026-07-21) Glean announced partnerships with GleekOps and LTM to accelerate enterprise AI adoption and integrate enterprise knowledge into agentic AI workflows, as reported by [EIN News](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMi1AFBVV95cUxOWDdNb3JCMkZIdE1ZWFlBbUJNd0E2QjNzQjlpa0V0bVlScVpfWGplWmwwQ19DT2t4NVdqWEl1UEV4R1RqR2d0Sld1TXJpajdzVlkzRkE1TkNEelYtc3c2dzlERkhrNXl1dEpxNVNnNFA4WlBuV3NaZTR6eE4yM091QURxZDVEbV9FNkc3Nmxsd19lUVB5TzNIX0N3cjlXRmdOejB2NHNEWWQtT2lRYTJ4TjRUUHp5eWxqSWZvbW40RXduR2NwRF9XQnd4TDRjU1VudXd4SQ?oc=5) and varindia.com. Additionally, a Glean report found that workers spend nearly a full day fixing AI mistakes, according to [The HR Digest](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMinwFBVV95cUxQUERIbXBoMi1PYks0bXkxQkg0aFVTakVuZ1lZUmNUdEhFa0FTcS1mYldsbGtTbHpMOFlPc0dQS0s1TnhTN0F1Q1FIdjVXRGRsR29zU1l1MEgxeWIxbUpIZG1OdVJoSm16d21nWHhzN1BZajNsYVBlZ1k5X29mbWdzUzA1RG5lWmNWWEhCSDU5cl8wRVNXTzU1bFF4MFFUTVU?oc=5).
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While Glean targets internal knowledge workers, its expansion into "agentic AI workflows" signals broader enterprise AI adoption. The reported time spent fixing AI mistakes is a direct opening to highlight Kore.ai's ABL for compile-time validation, deterministic execution, and robust governance, ensuring reliability for critical customer-facing interactions.

### Salesforce Agentforce targets SMBs and healthcare verticals
**What happened —** (2026-07-20) Salesforce launched an 'SMB Growth Kit' in India, integrating Agentforce with Slack and Tableau, as reported by NDTV Profit. Agentforce is also being highlighted for its role in shaping smart healthcare at EPCET Bangalore, according to Careers360.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This move indicates Salesforce's strategy to broaden Agentforce's market reach beyond large enterprises, leveraging its CRM ecosystem. However, it reinforces Agentforce's platform lock-in and the persistent challenge of unpredictable "Flex Credits" pricing, which Kore.ai can counter with its transparent, per-agent/intent/tool cost attribution and model-agnostic tiering.

### IBM expands AI platform into specialized training applications
**What happened —** (2026-07-21) Changi General Hospital and IBM are collaborating to build an AI-powered platform specifically for paediatric emergency training, as reported by The Edge Singapore.
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This initiative underscores IBM's continued focus on specialized, often internal, AI applications that typically require significant consulting and integration. It highlights IBM's position as a Gartner Challenger in Conversational AI, contrasting with Kore.ai's Leader status and broad, self-serviceable, omnichannel CX platform.
