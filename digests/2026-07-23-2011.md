# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Thursday, 23 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are aggressively pushing AI agent capabilities, with Salesforce and ServiceNow highlighting their platform integration and IBM focusing on a "control plane" approach. Kore.ai's ABL and proven analyst leadership remain key differentiators against these broad platform plays and emerging agent-focused vendors.

## Three signals that matter

### Salesforce Expands Agentforce to WhatsApp/SMS, Highlighting B2B Commerce
**What happened —** (2026-07-23) Salesforce launched a new B2B Commerce suite that embeds an AI buying agent directly into WhatsApp and SMS channels, aiming to streamline B2B purchasing journeys. MarketScale
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This move reinforces Salesforce's strategy of embedding agentic AI within its core CRM and commerce workflows, creating a potential lock-in for existing customers. Kore.ai's provider-agnostic approach and deep integration capabilities across any CRM or commerce platform offer a compelling alternative for enterprises seeking flexibility.

### ServiceNow CEO Emphasizes "Kill Switch" for AI Agents, Positioning for Control
**What happened —** (2026-07-23) ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott stated that the company has a "kill switch" to stop rogue AI agents, positioning ServiceNow as a leader in bringing "AI discipline" to enterprise AI chaos. TechRadar
**What it means for Kore.ai —** ServiceNow's focus on control and governance aligns with Kore.ai's ABL, which provides compile-time validation and deterministic execution. We can highlight our inherent "safety features" built into the platform, rather than as an add-on, contrasting with ServiceNow's emphasis on reactive "kill switches."

### IBM Positions watsonx Orchestrate as a Multi-Vendor "Agentic Control Plane"
**What happened —** (2026-07-23) IBM announced that its watsonx Orchestrate platform is being repositioned as a multi-vendor "agentic control plane," capable of orchestrating agents from various providers including Granite, Claude, GPT, and Mistral. [CX Today](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMimwFBVV95cUxNR1Fuc0JFM1B0WjMwNm84bDR3dXhaVmlMNkNIanNySHZIekdwR0IyT2pIdWFLYzF0SV9XdUZ4bDlETXhrb1hMejZXcllOU2w1VHZ6TzZsMW1DdXV1OWpHT3ZZUC1SWTVNa3pTLUdaSTdvWWpqWWxaSllPajRSRkFibm9SM1BMNldLS0hpTHJmNEJSakJyUVlLMkc4WQ?oc=5)
**What it means for Kore.ai —** IBM's strategy acknowledges the need for flexibility in model selection, a core tenet of Kore.ai's ABL. We can emphasize our established, provider-agnostic model tiering and routing capabilities, which offer a more mature and integrated "control plane" for enterprise CX than IBM's newer, multi-vendor orchestration approach.
