# Competitive Signal Report — Enterprise AI Platform (Friday, 24 July 2026)

> **Bottom line:** Competitors are aggressively pushing AI agent capabilities, with Salesforce and IBM highlighting platform integration and agent-assist respectively. Kore.ai's strength lies in its proven, analyst-validated platform depth and deterministic ABL, offering a clear counter to the emerging risks of consumption-based pricing and unproven AI governance in these broader platforms.

## Three signals that matter

### Salesforce Agentforce Crosses $1B ARR, Pushing Agentic Platform
**What happened —** Salesforce announced Agentforce ARR has crossed $1 billion, with a 205% YoY increase, and is committing $1 billion to expand Agentforce deployments in Switzerland over five years. [Salesforce](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMioAFBVV95cUxOQ0NhSk9XblBUcnNnSXU1QlEzVUZRYTdQdWlhcWw5bTd1eFVERDlzS2RhSUdSQXIyakpDVEtuYmRzc05IclBHVElHNlRGc3JmbGs0c2V5dGZ2M094WndDMEpWUWQwaTBQd3pnR2Z4N09TaF9vWFZKQm9xSkFoMHVUdUNLUlVlcGhJNVFXSEh2MWRIUzVkRDNsNEx1eGc2Mzd1?oc=5)
**What it means for Kore.ai —** While Agentforce's ARR growth is notable, its pricing model remains a significant risk. Kore.ai's ABL and compile-time validation offer superior TCO predictability and governance compared to Salesforce's consumption-based credits, which CIOs are wary of.

### IBM watsonx Orchestrate Focuses on Agentic Control Plane and Real-Time Data
**What happened —** IBM is emphasizing watsonx Orchestrate as a multi-vendor "agentic control plane" and highlighting the need for AI agents to have real-time data to deliver value, with agent assist for contact centers launching mid-2026. [IBM](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiswFBVV95cUxQUHRoNnJ6NlNIZmpQZGlBeDJyOWp5YVhPU09iZTN4ejFXS3lLTFJGaC0wVXo1QTI4R2hkZm5wNUlyWWhCU0VoRHkxd3NKU1k0WERpMGcxY0llT195Z3pOZHlmTFB1cGFaaU9ZMzNEZ1F3QlNveHRqcjZCeE81cTlVSmlwci0tU0lDOFI3bzg3TmxrTVVUdmpkY2JLbHR4b09yUmp4ZVVkR19GLU1fZU5jeExsSQ?oc=5)
**What it means for Kore.ai —** IBM's positioning as a "control plane" acknowledges the need for robust orchestration, a core strength of Kore.ai's ABL. Our platform's proven ability to integrate diverse models and provide deterministic execution addresses the inherent risks of a multi-vendor approach, especially for regulated industries.

### ServiceNow Positions Against Salesforce with AI-Powered CRM Expansion
**What happened —** ServiceNow is directly challenging Salesforce by leveraging its AI capabilities to expand its CRM offerings, emphasizing its SaaS strength. [CX Today](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMingFBVV95cUxOSjBidVU4T3E3cVMzN2ZxbjNiNDE3SjEtRGZjNzR4V3Y5dGh4c3ZHVlRjNmtqR2h0SGE1OXFlSGRnOXRkbFBNQm13U0lHSkUxNXRSOWVObHFKZUFCS3Z1bjhuWC1CQzF3VHZpVFRyR05uU2ZRQ0tGNU1OVEd0clpVbVJDNzUxQ0x2aU1HaFRhb0JKeXM0bmRyZjJaOWJSUQ?oc=5)
**What it means for Kore.ai —** This move underscores the broader trend of platform vendors expanding into agentic CX. Kore.ai's established leadership in Conversational AI and its comprehensive, analyst-validated platform provide a distinct advantage over ITSM-centric players like ServiceNow, particularly in true omnichannel customer engagement.

## Watch for
- Sierra's continued expansion and any analyst validation of their enterprise CX capabilities.
- Genesys's progress in integrating AI agents and the impact of their AWS collaboration.
- Further clarity on the integration and roadmap of Moveworks within ServiceNow.

## Recommended actions
- Emphasize Kore.ai's analyst-validated leadership in Conversational AI for Customer Service to counter the broad platform plays from Salesforce and ServiceNow.
- Highlight the TCO predictability and governance advantages of ABL over consumption-based credit models, especially for regulated industries.
- Arm Sales with specific questions around AI agent reliability, auditability, and cost forecasting for prospects evaluating Salesforce and IBM.
- Reinforce Kore.ai's true omnichannel capabilities against voice-first or digital-only offerings.
