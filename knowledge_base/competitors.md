## Cresta  · CX-native AI agents
**What it is:** Contact-center AI built on a **conversation-data moat** — years of
conversational intelligence (intent, agent behavior, resolution paths, workflow
friction) from millions of real conversations. Products span Insights, AI Analyst,
Quality Management, Automation Discovery, agent-assist, and AI agents.
- **June 11, 2026 — launched Cresta Conductor**, a developer-native environment to
  build/improve agents across a 4-stage lifecycle (Blueprint → Implement → Test &
  improve → Refine in production), incl. **AI Agent Testing 2.0** and synthetic
  customers. Cresta-stated ~50% faster build (internal FDE use, not customer-proven).
- **Forrester-cited** strengths in explainability (Wave: Conversation Intelligence,
  Q2 2025). Named customers: **United, Alaska, Cox**.
- **Our read (battlecard framing):** their data moat is the one thing ABL can't close
  by roadmap — concede it, reframe as a design input. We win on compile-time
  validation, deterministic execution, governance, and full-lifecycle platform depth.
  Their prompt-vs-code split is a manual call ("an IVR that calls an LLM" if over-coded);
  flagged on pricing complexity.
- **Watch for:** Conductor traction/customer proof, pricing changes, analyst moves,
  expansion beyond conversation intelligence into full agent build/deploy.

## PolyAI  · CX-native AI agents
**What it is:** **Voice-first / voice-only** customer-service automation, centered on
inbound voice.
- **Our read (battlecard framing):** narrower channel reach (lacks unified omnichannel
  across chat/messaging/digital); **consumption-based pricing** with fewer options to
  optimize cost; **higher build dependency** on PolyAI's own teams/roadmap; Gartner
  notes consumption-and-services pricing can make **long-term TCO less predictable**.
- **Watch for:** moves into digital/omnichannel, pricing changes, enterprise logos,
  funding, analyst placements.

## Parloa  · CX-native AI agents
**What it is:** Voice-first contact-center AI; Gartner describes it as an **"AI Agent
Management Platform"** with an agent-assist module (the **Arch** design layer).
- **Our read (battlecard framing):** **limited geographic reach** (customer base
  largely Europe — Gartner excluded it from the MQ on geographic presence); **not
  evaluated** by Gartner MQ, either Forrester Wave, either Everest PEAK, or IDC
  MarketScape; **almost no third-party proof** (≈1 verified G2 review). Narrower
  contact-center scope than a full enterprise agent platform.
- **Watch for:** US/enterprise expansion, first analyst evaluations, funding,
  big logos, third-party reviews accumulating.
