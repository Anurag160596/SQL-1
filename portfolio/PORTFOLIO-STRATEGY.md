# Portfolio strategy — PM / Strategy / Growth

Your positioning, the structure, and a concrete plan to turn the agents you're building into a portfolio recruiters take seriously. Target: **product, strategy, and growth roles** (blended).

---

## 1. The core insight

Most PM portfolios are a wall of screenshots and "I led X." Yours has something rarer: **working systems you built yourself, with real product judgment visible in them.** The risk is that recruiters read them as "engineering side-projects." The job of this portfolio is to reframe every agent as a **product** — problem, user, decisions, outcome — so the PM/strategy/growth signal lands instead of the code.

**Your one-line positioning:**

> *"I build AI agents that turn ambiguous problems into shipping systems that drive decisions."*

It works for all three role types: **Product** hears "ships, makes tradeoffs," **Strategy** hears "turns ambiguity into decisions," **Growth** hears "systems, leverage."

## 2. The narrative spine (use it everywhere)

Every agent becomes a case study with the same six beats. This consistency *is* the signal — it shows you think in a repeatable product framework, not one-off hacks.

1. **The opportunity** — the problem, sharpened. (Show the reframe.)
2. **Who it's for** — user + job-to-be-done.
3. **The product** — what it does, in their language.
4. **Key decisions & tradeoffs** — ← *the part most portfolios skip; it's where PM judgment shows.*
5. **How it works** — architecture at a glance, no code dump.
6. **Outcomes & what I'd measure next** — leading indicators, not just "I shipped it."

## 5. Distribution — where this actually goes

A portfolio only works if it's *seen*. Treat distribution like a growth funnel:

- **LinkedIn headline / featured:** pin the Artifact link. Headline something like *"PM/Strategy · I build AI agents that turn market noise into decisions."*
- **LinkedIn post:** one post per agent. Lead with the reframe (beat 1), show the attack→counter example, link the case study. This is your top-of-funnel.
- **Resume:** one line per agent under a "Projects" or "Selected work" section, each linking to its case study. *"Built an autonomous competitive-intelligence agent (live, 2×/day) that routes competitor moves to owning teams — 13 rivals tracked, 5-model resilient."*
- **Applications:** paste the case study into "tell us about a project" fields; drop the Artifact link everywhere a portfolio URL is asked for.
- **Cold outreach:** the case study *is* your reason to reach out to a PM/CI leader — "I built the thing your team does manually; 6-min read."
